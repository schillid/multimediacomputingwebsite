Most recent: Progress on the Subaru

I started working on the side of the car, back light and spoiler. I modelled after some reference pictures I found on Google Images.

Here I was confronted with a slight problem, I had to connect multiple edges coming from the top of the trunk to only two on the back but ...

... as you can see, I was able to connect them with quads only. On the right you can see the Turbosmooth isoline display preview.

Here you can see the finished side of the car. I also applied symmetry to the model, to see if everything is scaled properly.

I then created some floating geometry for the horizontal indentation in the door to create a nice normal map later then started working on the roof and windows.

The car's headlights and the frontbumper are done at this stage, I then started creating the geometry around the headlight.

I finished the front grill of the car end took a look at the car with a symmetry modifier applied. It shows that I still had to work on the polygon flow down the middle of the car where the seam for the symmerty is. I also added my finished side mirrors.

I finished the wheels and did some last touch ups on the car's geometry. The polygon flow for some parts was not perfect yet at this point which was easy to see after I bumped up the Turbosmooth setting. (Note the two thick lines going down the hood.)

Here you can see the beginning of the retopology of the car. You can see the wheel and spoiler I created properly here too. I am using 3ds Max's PolyDraw tools to retopologize the car.

On the right: the finished,turbosmoothed car. Ready for normals baking. On the left: The finished lowpoly model of the Subaru. I applied a simple flatten mapping the the car to see which areas needed work which was all of them.

Based on the low poly model I then created the projection cage for raycasting. I was able to create a normals and an ambient occlusion map.
